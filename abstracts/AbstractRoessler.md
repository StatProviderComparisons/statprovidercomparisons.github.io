This presentation introduces the methodology of Multilevel Quality Indicators (MQI). The MQI methodology facilitates the derivation of directly standardized quality indicators for health service providers and geographical regions based on a statistical multilevel model. This model may include characteristics of both, health service providers and regions. We investigate the statistical properties of MQI using Monte Carlo simulations and compare them with the properties of established approaches, including estimation of standardized mortality ratios (SMRs) and risk-standardized mortality rates (RSMRs). The simulation results indicate clear advantages of MQI over established approaches in terms of quality-based ranking of health care providers, mainly due to increased statistical efficiency. We illustrate these advantages using real-world data on patients receiving thoracic surgical treatment of lung cancer in German hospitals.
